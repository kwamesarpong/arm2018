VISA SECTION

Please be aware that information presented hereafter are for general information only and do not at any time replace the information available on the Ghana Immigration Service website or any of the information provided by an officer of a visa office.

While the OC will do everything in its power to provide timely information and to facilitate processes for international delegates, it is important to understand that the due process of visa applications will need to be respected.



Obtaining a Ghana visa

All foreigners seeking permission to enter Ghana require Ghana entry visa unless otherwise exempted. The visa should be endorsed on valid passport or travel documents issued by competent national or international authorities and recognized by the Government of Ghana.

Ghana entry visas are obtainable from any Ghana Embassy, High Commission or Consulate abroad.

Ghana visas — General information

  • Visas may be granted to personnel of international organisations and visitors to Ghana for tourism, study, business or transit.
  • The visa may be granted for single or multiple entry.
  • Visas are valid for a period of three months and must be utilised within three months of the date of issue. Visas valid for up to one year may be granted for specific purposes.
  • Single-entry visa is valid for 90 days from the date of issue. Please apply only within 90 days of the date of travel.
  • Ghana multiple-entry visa should be requested by writing a separate letter addressed to the embassy of Ghana explaining the reason for multiple entries.
  • All travelers aged nine (9) months and above, coming into, or transiting through Ghana are required to have been vaccinated against Yellow Fever at least ten (10) days from the proposed date of travel and where already vaccinated, provide evidence (certificate) indicating the duration of a vaccination status of not more than ten (10) years before entering Ghana. Only yellow fever vaccination is obligatory.
  • Your passport must have at least 6 months of validity remaining when you apply for a visa.
  • Persons who enter the country as visitors are not permitted to take up employment even if there happens to be a vacancy on the Immigrant Quota of a prospective employer.



Emergency visa (visa-on-arrival)

Where Ghana has no Consular or Diplomatic representation entry visa may be issued on arrival, if the Director of Immigration has prior notification from travelers or their sponsors or hosts and approval has been granted. More on Emergency Visa (also known as ‘Visa on Arrival’): Click here.



Visa requirements

  • Visa Application Forms to be completed and signed
  • Colour Passport size photos (2) to be pasted or stapled in designated place on application form
  • Applicants on a VISIT to Ghana must provide a letter of invitation from their host in Ghana.
  • Visitors travelling on Business are required to furnish evidence of the fact, such as a letter from their firm, while those travelling at the invitation of Ghanaian nationals or residence or Ghanaian institutions should furnish a letter of invitation from their hosts.
  • Business, Residential addresses and contact telephone numbers to be provided
  • Copy or proof of round-trip tickets or itinerary
  • Copy of a recent bank statement showing sufficient funds for travel to Ghana
  • Occupation
  • Financial means to be indicated
  • Proposed date of travel
  • Names and Addresses of references and/ Accommodation in Ghana (if it is a hotel, details are to be provided)
  • Letter of consent from parent/legal guardian for children under 18 years



Travelers are strongly advised to ensure that they have all the necessary documents before arriving in Ghana

FGMSA

The Federation of Ghana Medical Students’ Association is the association of medical and dental students in Ghana and caters to the thousands of medical students in the country. FGMSA shares in the mission of the IFMSA and offers future physicians the opportunity to start making a difference in the world now.


IFMSA

The International Federation of Medical Students’ Associations (IFMSA), founded in 1951, is one of the world’s oldest and largest student-run organisations. It represents, connects and engages daily with an inspiring and engaging network of 1.3 million medical students from 137 National Member Organisations in 127 countries around the globe.
